Why does this happen?

It is usually one of the following: your local SOCKS5 proxy helper (port 9151) or the underlying arti daemon (port 9150) crashed or refused to start. It could be that you are completely offline (or Tor is blocked by your ISP), or the target service itself is dead. Or, because we route traffic through a complex multi-hop overlay transport, the Tor circuit build failed or timed out. Alternatively, WebKit might have been too impatient and decided not to wait for the proxy handshake to finish before giving up.

What does this actually mean?

Rather than connecting directly, Juanita routes requests through local daemons and onion relays to mask your network signature. When any link in this chain breaks—whether it's our internal proxy helper, a local Tor node, or the destination server—the whole request drops dead. You can try checking the Tor / Handshake status in juanita://config, or making sure you have an active network connection.
