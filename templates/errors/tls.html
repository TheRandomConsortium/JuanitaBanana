HTTPS Does Not Fly Here Son

Why does this happen?

Could be any of the following: a random developer who forgot to set up their certificates, or was too lazy to bother. It could be ICANN tyranny — the centralised certificate authority cartel simply doesn't issue certs for domains outside their monopoly (hi, Handshake). It could be a malicious actor trying to intercept your traffic. Or — in 99.99% of cases — it's just someone whose certbot forgot to autorenew and the cert expired at 3am on a Sunday.

What does this actually mean?

TLS/SSL encrypts your connection in transit — it stops your ISP or a café router from reading the raw bytes going by. What it doesn't do is tell you the server is trustworthy, or that the software running on it is safe, or that the company behind it isn't selling your data anyway. The certificate just means someone paid $0 to Let's Encrypt and ran one command. The padlock icon was always theatre — it protects the wire, not the destination. Connecting via HTTP exposes your traffic to passive interception on the network path, which on Tor or a VPN is already largely mitigated. Decide accordingly.
